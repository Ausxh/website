The Tower of Terror: Early Playtesting

2026-04-05

The Tower of Terror is supposed to be a 2D platformer game where the player is a human trying to make their way to the top of an alien tower. They progress through various rooms with platforming elements while avoiding obstacles and dealing with enemies.

My role in the development of this game has been to be the programmer and designer. As a result, I’ve been doing some programming and have been keeping track of the design document for the game. After a decent amount of work, we finally got something that was vaguely playable (although with only one level).

A while ago, we had a playtesting session in class, where people tried our game out and gave us feedback.

The game was pretty raw, so there wasn’t that much to say. However, it seems like people like the art style, even though I was slightly concerned it might not be readable. That’s cool! Also, two of the playtesters mentioned that they liked the mechanic where the player sticks to walls. This is a bug that I had actually been planning to fix, but I might try and keep it as a feature since people seemed to like it.

Another thing we’re planning on changing is actually the platformer aspect, as we realized the scope was too large for the deadline we had. Playtesters also gave the feedback of the goal being unclear, which requires good level design. Instead, the game will be changing into a small one-level battle arena, where the player can shoot enemies that spawn periodically.
